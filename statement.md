
Project Statement: Bio-Inspired Self-Healing Micro-Lattices
1. The Clinical Gap: "The Stress Shielding Paradox"
Current orthopedic surgery relies heavily on solid metallic implants (Titanium alloys and Stainless Steel). While these materials offer high mechanical strength, they introduce a fundamental biomechanical conflict: Stress Shielding.
 * The Problem: Natural bone is a dynamic, living tissue that requires mechanical loading to maintain density (Wolff’s Law). Solid metal implants are significantly stiffer than bone, absorbing the majority of the load.
 * The Consequence: The surrounding bone, deprived of mechanical stimulus, undergoes resorption (atrophy). This leads to implant loosening, periprosthetic fractures, and the eventual necessity for high-risk revision surgeries.
2. The Technical Challenge: Fatigue and Failure
Beyond stiffness mismatch, traditional implants are "static." Over millions of cycles (walking, running, climbing), micro-cracks inevitably form due to material fatigue. In a biological system, these cracks would trigger a cellular remodeling response; in a synthetic implant, they propagate until catastrophic failure.
Current Limitations:
 * Zero Plasticity: No mechanism to arrest crack growth at the micro-scale.
 * Poor Osseointegration: Solid surfaces do not allow for deep bone ingrowth or nutrient transport.
 * High Revision Rates: Approximately 10-15% of joint replacements require revision within 20 years, largely due to mechanical loosening.
3. The Proposed Solution: Bio-SHM
This project proposes a Bio-inspired Self-Healing Micro-lattice (Bio-SHM). By shifting the design paradigm from solid mass to architected cellular solids, we aim to synchronize the implant's mechanical properties with the host tissue.
Key Objectives:
 * Modulus Matching: Utilizing Gyroid TPMS (Triply Periodic Minimal Surfaces) to tune the Young’s Modulus (E) of the implant to match cortical (15-20 \text{ GPa}) and cancellous bone (0.1-2 \text{ GPa}).
 * Autonomous Repair: Integrating a vascularized network of micro-capsules containing bio-compatible monomers. Upon structural compromise, these capsules rupture to "heal" the crack, mimicking the biological healing process of bone.
 * Enhanced Porosity: Maintaining a 70-80% open-cell porosity to facilitate osteoblast migration and long-term biological fixation.
4. Expected Impact
By successfully merging Additive Manufacturing (SLS) with Biomimetic Geometry, the Bio-SHM project aims to:
 * Extend the lifespan of orthopedic implants by >50%.
 * Eliminate the primary cause of aseptic loosening.
 * Reduce the patient recovery period through superior initial osseointegration.
