

Project Overview
The Bio-SHM project addresses the critical failure points of traditional orthopedic implants: structural rigidity and fatigue-induced loosening. By leveraging Triply Periodic Minimal Surfaces (TPMS)—specifically Gyroid geometries—this project develops a synthetic micro-lattice that mimics the trabecular (spongy) structure of human bone.
The innovation lies in its dual-functionality:
 * Bio-Mechanical Matching: Reducing the Young’s Modulus mismatch to prevent bone resorption.
 * Autonomous Repair: Utilizing micro-encapsulated bio-compatible polymers that rupture upon micro-crack formation, restoring structural integrity without surgical intervention.

 Key Features
1. Biomimetic Geometry (TPMS)
 * Gyroid Architecture: Optimized surface-to-volume ratios that provide uniform stress distribution and 70-80% porosity.
 * Osseointegration: The porous nature facilitates nutrient flow and provides a scaffold for natural bone ingrowth.
2. Autonomous Self-Healing Mechanism
 * Micro-channel Integration: A vascular-like network within the lattice filled with bio-compatible monomers.
 * Crack-Triggered Polymerization: When a micro-fracture occurs, the healing agent is released, reacting to fill the void and arrest crack propagation.
3. Advanced Simulation & Modeling
 * FEA Stress Heat-Mapping: Validated using Ansys to ensure peak stresses are redistributed away from the bone-implant interface.
 * Gait Simulation: Designed to withstand 10^7 cycles of cyclic loading, approximating years of human movement.
4. Digital-to-Physical Workflow
 * Algorithmic Design: Generated via Rhino and Grasshopper for precise control over lattice density gradients.
 * AM Compatible: Specifically optimized for Selective Laser Sintering (SLS) using Ti-6Al-4V (Grade 5 Titanium).

 Tech Stack
 * Modeling: Rhino 7, Grasshopper (Lattice optimization)
 * Simulation: Ansys (Finite Element Analysis), COMSOL (Fluid dynamics for healing agents)
 * Material Science: Ti-6Al-4V Alloy, Bio-epoxy monomers
 * Documentation: LaTeX (for technical reporting), Markdown

 Performance Metrics
 * Weight Reduction: 40% lighter than solid titanium counterparts.
 * Strength Retention: Only a 5% loss in Ultimate Tensile Strength (UTS) despite high porosity.
 * Recovery Rate: Measurement of ~85% structural strength recovery within 24 hours of a micro-cracking event.

System Architecture
The project follows a layered architectural approach:
 * Modeling Layer: CAD generation of TPMS.
 * Simulation Layer: Stress and strain verification.
 * Synthesis Layer: Additive manufacturing and infusion.

 Future Roadmap
 * 4D Printing: Integrating Shape Memory Alloys (SMAs) that adapt to body temperature.
 * Smart Monitoring: Piezoelectric sensors to transmit "implant health" data to a surgeon’s dashboard.
